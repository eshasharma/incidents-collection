Symantec to Buy Software Tracker

By BLOOMBERG NEWSJAN. 30, 2007



Continue reading the main story
Share This Page

Continue reading the main story








Symantec,  a maker of computer antivirus products,  will buy Altiris  for about $1.03 billion to gain programs that track corporate software use.Altiris shareholders will receive $33 a share in cash, Symantec, which is based in Cupertino, Calif., said yesterday in a statement. That is 22 percent more than Altiris’s closing price of $27.14 on Friday. The purchase price includes about $200 million Altiris has  in cash on hand and short-term investments.Altiris products help clients track, install and maintain their software on desktops, laptops, servers and hand-held devices. Symantec is expanding its offerings  after posting  slower sales of storage software to large customers.
