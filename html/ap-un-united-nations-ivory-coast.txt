Africa 
UN Security Council Condemns Ivory Coast Attack

By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 14, 2016, 2:28 P.M. E.D.T.



Continue reading the main story
Share This Page

Continue reading the main story








UNITED NATIONS —  The United Nations Security Council is condemning the attacks in Ivory Coast over the weekend that left 18 people dead, including 15 civilians.The council in a statement Monday called for the perpetrators and organizers of the attack to be brought to justice. Al-Qaida in the Islamic Maghreb, known as AQIM, has claimed responsibility.Sunday's attack left the resort town of Grand-Bassam and all of Ivory Coast reeling from its first Islamic extremist attack. Grand-Bassam is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a popular destination for Ivorians and foreigners.President Alassane Ouattara's government began work Monday to tighten security and prevent similar violence.
