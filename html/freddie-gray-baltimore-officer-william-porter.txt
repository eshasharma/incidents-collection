Officer Charged in Freddie Gray Case Ordered to Testify Against Co-Defendants

By SHERYL GAY STOLBERGMARCH 8, 2016

WASHINGTON — Maryland’s highest court on Tuesday ordered a Baltimore police officer charged in the death of Freddie Gray to testify against five of his fellow officers, clearing the way for the state to move ahead with long-stalled prosecutions in a case that set off racial unrest last year.

Court Blocks Order That Officer Testify in Freddie Gray CaseJAN. 8, 2016







In a simple four-paragraph order that did not lay out the court’s reasoning, the Maryland Court of Appeals announced its decision to compel Officer William G. Porter to testify. The court said the ruling would be explained in an opinion to be issued later.Officer Porter’s trial on charges of manslaughter and assault in the death of Mr. Gray, a 25-year-old black man who sustained a fatal spinal cord injury in police custody, ended with a hung jury in December. The state sought to retry Officer Porter in June while first compelling him to testify against the others.Officer Porter’s lawyers fought the move, leading to delays in the other trials.
