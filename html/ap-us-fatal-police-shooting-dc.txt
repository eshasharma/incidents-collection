Police: Officer Fatally Shoots Man in Northeast Washington

By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 1, 2016, 9:39 A.M. E.S.T.


Inside









Advertisement

Continue reading the main story





Continue reading the main story

WASHINGTON —  Police in the District of Columbia say an officer fatally shot a man in northeast Washington.Police spokesman Dustin Sternbeck said Monday that the officer tried to stop a man around 2 a.m. and the man engaged the officer in a physical altercation. Sternbeck says the man brandished a gun, prompting the officer to fire his service weapon, striking the man. He says the man was taken to a hospital, where he died.Sternbeck says there's no report of an injury to the officer. He says the man's gun was recovered at the scene.Sternbeck did not have any details about why the officer tried to stop the man.
