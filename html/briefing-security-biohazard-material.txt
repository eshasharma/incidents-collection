BRIEFING: SECURITY
BRIEFING: SECURITY; BIOHAZARD MATERIAL


By Jo Piazza


Published: August 11, 2002









                    State Public Safety and Health officials met with scientists from Rutgers University and the University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey last week to begin developing a plan for guidelines to protect the security of shipments of anthrax and other biohazardous research materials across the state. Citing the need to protect those shipments from becoming the target of a terrorist attack, the state health commissioner, Clifton Lacy, said police escorts could be among the committee's recommendations, expected to be released by Oct. 1.   Jo Piazza
